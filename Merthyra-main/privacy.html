Merthyra Privacy Policy

This policy explains how Merlin Mikulas, Merthyra’s independent developer and website operator, handles information when you visit merthyra.com or use the Merthyra public beta, and the limited circumstances in which its publisher may receive information.

Effective and last updated: July 22, 2026

Plain-language summary: Merlin Mikulas operates the Merthyra website and develops the app. Mikulas Entertainment LLC publishes Merthyra but does not operate this website. Merthyra is designed as a local-first workspace: core workspace content stays on your device and, when enabled, in your private Apple iCloud container. Neither the Developer nor Publisher sells your data or receives your workspace content merely because you use the app.

1. Scope and who we are

This Privacy Policy applies to Merthyra beta applications, the website at merthyra.com, beta documentation, and feedback you send directly to us. Merlin Mikulas is Merthyra’s independent developer and the operator of this website (the “Developer,” “we,” “us,” or “our”).

Mikulas Entertainment LLC is Merthyra’s publisher (the “Publisher”). The Publisher may provide publishing, distribution, marketing, business, and legal support under a separate agreement with the Developer, but it does not operate merthyra.com merely because it publishes the app. The Publisher does not automatically receive Merthyra workspace content or website data.

This policy does not govern Apple, TestFlight, iCloud, MapKit, GitHub, or other third-party services. Those providers process information under their own privacy notices and terms.

2. Privacy summary

  • No Merthyra account is required for the current public beta.
  • Core workspace data is local-first and may synchronize through your private iCloud account when iCloud Drive is enabled.
  • We do not sell personal information and do not use workspace content for targeted advertising.
  • We do not train a developer-operated AI model on your workspace content.
  • Permissions are feature-specific. Calendar, location, notifications, Siri, and similar access depend on the features you choose and permissions you grant.
  • Apple may provide limited TestFlight diagnostics such as sessions, crashes, and feedback under Apple’s systems and your choices.

3. Workspace content processed by the app

Depending on the features you use, Merthyra may process content such as:

  • Documents, notes, writing-analysis results, projects, collections, research, references, and files.
  • Calendar records, event details, recurrence rules, alerts, time zones, attendees, URLs, and stored event locations.
  • Assistant profiles, names, roles, bios, descriptions, tones, personalities, custom instructions, profile pictures, memories, conversations, and retrieval indexes.
  • Imported assistant data or conversation histories, including content extracted from a supported export file.
  • App settings, themes, editor preferences, layout choices, sidebar configuration, sorting, and startup-model preferences.

This information is processed to provide the features you request. Core workspace content is stored on your device and, when configured, in Merthyra’s container within your iCloud account. Neither the Developer nor Publisher receives or has access to that content merely because you use Merthyra.

4. Calendar, notifications, maps, and location

Apple Calendar

If you choose to import Apple Calendar information, Merthyra requests calendar access through Apple’s EventKit framework. You choose which available calendars to import. Imported event information is copied into your Merthyra workspace. The current import feature is designed not to modify or delete source events in Apple Calendar.

Maps and places

When you search for a place or display a map, search text, map region, or related requests may be sent to Apple through MapKit. If you choose “Use My Current Location,” “Search Near Me,” or a similar feature, your device may provide precise location to support that request. Merthyra asks for location permission at the time a location-aware feature is used.

Selected event locations may include a display name, an address you enter or edit, coordinates, an Apple Place ID, a time-zone identifier, and whether the location is a custom pin. That event information may be saved with your Merthyra calendar and synchronized through iCloud.

Notifications

If you enable alerts, Merthyra uses Apple’s local notification system to schedule reminders on your device. Notification permissions and delivery are controlled by your operating-system settings.

5. Assistants, AI features, and imported histories

Merthyra may process your workspace content locally to search, retrieve relevant information, compose responses, save memories, and perform supported actions. Merthyra Lloer and imported assistant profiles use Merthyra’s local assistant and retrieval systems. Their profiles, memories, indexes, avatars, and conversations may be stored in your Merthyra workspace and synchronized through iCloud.

On supported devices, you may choose Apple’s on-device Foundation Model. Requests to that model are handled through Apple’s Foundation Models framework and are subject to Apple’s device availability, software, and privacy practices. Merthyra does not send model prompts or responses to a Developer- or Publisher-operated server merely because you use that feature.

If you import a third-party assistant export or conversation history, the import can contain sensitive information. Merthyra processes the file to extract supported profile, memory, and conversation content and creates local retrieval data. Review your export before importing it, and remove content you do not want stored or synchronized.

Merthyra does not use your private workspace, assistant conversations, memories, or imports to train a developer-operated advertising or general-purpose AI model.

6. iCloud, device services, and Apple processing

If iCloud Drive is enabled for Merthyra, supported data may be placed in your private iCloud container so it can synchronize across devices signed in to the same Apple Account. Apple controls the iCloud infrastructure, encryption, account authentication, regional processing, storage limits, backups, and service availability.

Merthyra may also use Apple frameworks for maps, calendar permissions, notifications, Siri, Shortcuts, App Intents, image selection, and on-device intelligence. Information provided to or received from those services is handled under Apple’s applicable privacy policy and terms. Permissions are controlled in your Apple device settings.

7. TestFlight beta information

Apple operates TestFlight. Depending on Apple’s settings and your choices, Apple may collect or provide beta-related information such as device and operating-system details, installation status, sessions, crashes, diagnostics, screenshot feedback, and written feedback.

When a tester joins through a public TestFlight link, Apple may display the tester as anonymous to us rather than providing the tester’s name and email address. Apple’s processing is governed by Apple’s TestFlight terms and privacy practices. Do not include private workspace content or sensitive information in feedback unless necessary and you intend to share it.

8. Website and communication information

Website visits

The Merthyra website does not currently include a Merthyra-operated account system, advertising tracker, behavioral analytics service, or marketing cookie. The site’s hosting and network providers may automatically process ordinary technical information—such as IP address, browser type, requested page, date and time, and security logs—to deliver and protect the website under their own policies.

Consent checkbox

The public beta consent checkbox is processed in your browser only to enable the TestFlight button. The current website does not send that checkbox state to the Developer or Publisher or store it in a cookie or browser database.

Feedback and communications

If you contact the Developer, post on a forum, open a GitHub issue, or otherwise submit feedback, we process the information you choose to provide, such as your contact details, message, screenshots, diagnostic files, and attachments, to respond, troubleshoot, improve Merthyra, and maintain appropriate records. Public forum posts and GitHub issues may be visible to others. Relevant feedback may be shared with the Publisher when reasonably necessary for publishing, distribution, support, security, or legal matters.

9. How information is used

Where applicable, information is used to:

  • Provide, maintain, secure, troubleshoot, and improve Merthyra and the website.
  • Save, synchronize, retrieve, display, import, export, and organize the content you choose.
  • Run assistant, calendar, mapping, notification, Siri, Shortcut, and writing features you request.
  • Respond to questions, feedback, legal requests, and support or safety concerns.
  • Detect abuse, investigate crashes or security incidents, enforce applicable terms, and comply with law.

Depending on where you live, the Developer’s processing may be based on providing the service you requested, your consent, legitimate interests in operating and securing Merthyra, or compliance with legal obligations. You may withdraw permission for optional device access through system settings, although affected features may stop working.

10. When information may be shared

We do not sell personal information and do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. We may disclose information in these limited circumstances:

  • At your direction: when you export, share, publish, or send content, or choose an Apple integration.
  • Service providers: to providers that host, protect, or support the website and communications, subject to their roles and appropriate obligations.
  • Publisher: the Developer may provide the Publisher with information reasonably necessary for publishing, distributing, marketing, supporting, securing, or legally protecting Merthyra. This does not give the Publisher automatic access to private local or iCloud workspace content.
  • Apple services: when iCloud, TestFlight, MapKit, EventKit, notifications, Siri, Shortcuts, or Foundation Models are used.
  • Legal and safety reasons: when reasonably necessary to comply with law, legal process, enforce rights, investigate fraud or abuse, or protect people, Merthyra, the Developer, the Publisher, or the public.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, financing, acquisition, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, subject to applicable law.

Because core workspace data resides on your device and in your iCloud account, neither the Developer nor Publisher can disclose private workspace content they do not possess.

11. Storage, retention, and deletion

Workspace content remains on your device and, if enabled, in iCloud until you delete it, remove the relevant Merthyra data, or Apple removes it under your account settings. Deleting the app may not delete synchronized iCloud files or device backups.

You can delete documents, events, assistant profiles, memories, conversations, imported histories, avatars, and other supported data through Merthyra’s controls or by managing the relevant Merthyra files in iCloud where appropriate. Allow time for deletions to synchronize across devices.

Communications and feedback received by the Developer, or shared with or sent directly to the Publisher, are retained only as long as reasonably necessary for support, product improvement, publishing, security, legal compliance, and recordkeeping. TestFlight data is retained by Apple according to Apple’s policies and controls. If you ask the Developer or Publisher to delete information they control, limited records may be retained where required by law or necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

12. Security and beta limitations

We use reasonable administrative and technical measures appropriate to the information we control. However, no device, storage system, transmission method, or beta software is completely secure. The public beta may contain security flaws, and we cannot guarantee absolute security, uninterrupted synchronization, or data preservation.

Protect your Apple Account and devices, use available passcodes and authentication controls, keep software updated, review imported histories, and maintain independent backups. Do not store passwords, authentication secrets, payment-card details, or other highly sensitive information in the beta.

13. Children’s privacy

Merthyra and its public beta are intended for people age 13 and older and are not directed to children under 13. The Developer does not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 provided personal information to the Developer or Publisher, contact us so the matter can be reviewed and appropriate action taken.

14. International use

Information sent directly to the Developer or Publisher may be processed in the United States or wherever their respective service providers operate. Apple and other third parties determine the locations in which they process information under their own privacy terms. Where required, applicable safeguards and legal mechanisms are used for transfers of information each party controls.

15. Your choices and privacy rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to request access to, correction of, deletion of, or a copy of personal information that the Developer or Publisher controls, and to object to or restrict certain processing. You may also have the right to appeal a response or complain to a privacy regulator.

For app data stored only on your device or in your private iCloud account, use Merthyra, Files, iCloud, and Apple device controls because neither the Developer nor Publisher possesses that content. You can:

  • Change calendar, location, notification, Siri, and other permissions in system settings.
  • Disable iCloud access for Merthyra, subject to possible effects on synchronization.
  • Delete supported content in Merthyra and remove local or iCloud files where appropriate.
  • Leave the TestFlight beta and manage TestFlight choices through Apple.
  • Contact the Developer about information you provided through Merthyra or merthyra.com, or contact the Publisher about information you sent directly to it.

We may need to verify your request and may decline or limit a request where permitted by law.

16. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as Merthyra changes. The date at the top indicates the latest revision. Material changes may also be announced on the website, in TestFlight notes, or in the app. If a change requires consent, we will request it where required by law.
